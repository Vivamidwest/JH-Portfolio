Josh Hunt - Project Case Study

**Project Title:** WandaVision (Marvel Studios) **Role:** Visual Effects Editor **Company/Client:** Marvel Studios / Disney+ **Dates:** November 2019 - April 2020 **Project Type:** High-Profile TV Series

1. Project Overview & Context

Summary: WandaVision was a groundbreaking Marvel Studios series for Disney+, known for its innovative storytelling and ambitious visual effects that evolved through different eras of television.

Overall Contribution: As a Visual Effects Editor, I was integral to the post-production pipeline, ensuring the seamless integration and meticulous tracking of thousands of VFX shots. Furthermore, I was the sole member of the editorial crew located at The Walt Disney Company in Burbank for four months, while everyone else was at the studio in Atlanta, pre-pandemic.

View the Official Trailer: For a glimpse of the series' visual scope, you can watch the WandaVision Official Trailer on YouTube.

2. My Role

Specific Responsibilities:

  • Managed 10 simultaneous visual effects projects, between three editorial teams, demonstrating exceptional organizational skills under high pressure.
  • Meticulously tracked and integrated 200+ visual effects shots per episode, using a FileMaker Pro database, enhancing team efficiency and focus, and ensuring proper version control and implementation into final cuts.
  • Conducted an exhaustive compilation of all supplemental elements to support the successful outcome of each visual effect shot.
  • Prepared offline elements for post-visualization.
  • Created minor temp effect compositions to minimize visual dissonance and demonstrate the intended purpose of specific visual effects shots.

Example: The first image contained a green screen on the computer monitor. I created a rough chroma key for temp viewing. The second image is the final aired version.

3. Process & Challenges

Workflow Description: My process involved identifying and labeling individual effects shots, collaborating with editorial teams, liaising with VFX vendors, updating the FileMaker database, and preparing sequences for post-visualization.

Challenges Faced:

  • Challenge 1: High Volume & Complexity: Navigating an unprecedented volume of diverse VFX shots, while coordinating with remote colleagues.
    • Solution: Implemented rigorous organizational systems and proactive communication protocols to manage concurrent project demands effectively.
  • Challenge 2: Security & Confidentiality: Operating within strict studio security protocols and Non-Disclosure Agreements (NDAs) while maintaining efficient data flow.
    • Solution: Developed secure internal workflows for asset management and version control, ensuring compliance without hindering production timelines.
  • Challenge 3: Rapid Iteration: Adapting quickly to frequent creative changes and technical adjustments during the post-production cycle.
    • Solution: Leveraged advanced software proficiency and a flexible approach to rapidly implement revisions and maintain project momentum.

Example: Diagram illustrating a simplified VFX pipeline, highlighting my role in data flow and quality control.

4. Impact & Outcome

Quantifiable Results:

  • Contributed directly to the successful delivery of all nine episodes of a critically acclaimed series, recognized with VES and Emmy nominations for Visual Effects.
  • My workflow optimizations led to an average efficiency increase of 2 hours saved per day for the editorial team.
  • Ensured precise integration of visual effects, maintaining the high aesthetic and narrative standards expected of a Marvel production.

Overall Learning/Growth: This project significantly advanced my expertise in large-scale VFX pipeline management, security protocols, and adaptability to alternative workflows.
